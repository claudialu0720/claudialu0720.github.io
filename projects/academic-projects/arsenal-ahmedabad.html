Arsenal: Ahmedabad Building School
 Fall 2018

The expansion of urban development is constantly ongoing in developing countries. We, as future architects, are facing this challenge that the urban space needs not only the expected outcomes but also the unpredicted conditions that the city might encounter in the future.

This project experiments the scheme of “incremental design“ (“building the unbuilt“), to accommodate any future situation that might be brought by various stakeholders in the city of Ahmedabad, India.

Images

The current layout of the resources in the city suggests the disconnection between traditional building techniques (old city) and the advanced design theories and technologies (institutes).

By initiating the program as a Building school, this project will collect the resources together to the site as an urban arsenal, which can sustain the culture of the city with innovated experiments and curricula.

The project site, located on the western bank of the Sabarmati River in the relatively affluent area of Paldi, contains Le Corbusier's Sanskar Kendra and Doshi's Tagore Hall.

The immediate context includes the newly redeveloped Sabarmati Riverfront Park to the east and the National Institute of Design to the south. Also present to the west are the historic neighborhoods of Paldi and Kocharab, the latter including Kocharab Ashram - Mahatma Gandhi's residence and school.
