Split 3.0: An Urban Sancturary
 Spring 2019

This project explores the possibility of collective living among stray cats, Airbnb owners, college students, tourists, and residents through the design of three housing complexes with collective commons at the University of Split, Croatia.

Concept

Inspired by contemporary shared housing concepts, the housing design has flexible sleeping arrangements enabled by sliding wall panels.

The built-in furniture on the periphery of the building not only maximizes the flexibility of the interior space, but also acts as a passive design system that shades the summer sun with its particular orientation.

Each floor's communal space is located at the triangular area that is defined by the service elements, including stairs, bathrooms, kitchens, and load-bearing walls.
