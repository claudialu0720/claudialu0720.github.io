Diction Analysis 1
Song: King of Rock
Artists: Run D.M.C
link
This diction analysis diagrams the structure of the song chronologically by mapping its rhyme schemes and sectional breaks. Black bars represent the temporal placement and rhythm of rhymes, while arrows indicate echoing effects. Most sections follow a four-line pattern, with curved dotted lines above and straight lines below connecting recurring rhymes or instances of alliteration, revealing underlying linguistic patterns and sonic continuity.

Diction Analysis 2
Song: Public Enemy No.1
Artists: Public Enemy
link
Upon first listening to the track, I envisioned it as an expanding organism—growing with each repetition of the MCs' refrain: “Number one, one, one...”. This sense of rhythmic accumulation is visualized through a spiral diagram, symbolizing dynamic growth. Originating from the center, the spiral branches out with primary rhymes and secondary sub-rhymes, forming thresholds at key refrains and capturing the song’s layered momentum.

Diction Analysis 3
Song: Eric B is President
Artists: Eric B and Rakim
link
While analyzing the song’s diction, I came to realize that a purely rational and chronological approach was insufficient. The rhymes are often fragmentary, abstract, and resist systematic categorization. In response, I shifted toward a more interpretive, deconstructive method—highlighting major rhyme moments with circular markers and connecting them with lines. These connections form the visual and conceptual pathways of the track, allowing meaning to emerge through pattern recognition rather than strict sequencing.
