Drawring structure

  1. Shapes extracted from the graffiti collections by only seeing graphic objects as shapes but not as walls, floors, ramps etc.
  2. Glyphic language created from the shape language communicates in simplified terms. Typography used as a suggestive sign without specific referent.
  3. Patterns and grids printed on graphix sheets with expressive brush strokes can be found on this layer.
  4. Graffiti trains documented by Henry Chalfant and acetone transferred images on a sheet of Bristol paper as the base.
