Images

(Left: Temporal Diagram) A visual analysis of the hip-hop song “Freedom” by Grandmaster Flash and the Furious Five, one of the genre’s earliest party tracks from the 1980s. The diagram maps the emotional energy of the song over time, with the x-axis representing chronology and the y-axis capturing the intensity of atmosphere, simulating the euphoric progression of a party.

(Right: Found-Object Translation) Three abstract models reinterpret key poetic elements of the song using found materials—computer parts, wood, and glass. Each composition translates rhythm, layering, and tonal contrast into tactile form, exploring the spatial potential of sound. The plan and section drawings of the found-object models serve as the foundation for developing my graphic language.

The ‘children drawing’ introduces a distinctive zigzag geometry, which is further developed in the Relief Model. These shapes are digitized in Rhino, where they are lofted, booleaned, and recombined to explore spatial and formal variation.

3D printed model generated from 2D diagrams from the song “Freedom”.
